  • University of Copenhagen:
    Fall Engineering Exchange

    "The Copenhagen Engineering Exchange program is actually located in Lyngby, Denmark! The location is misadvertised, but Denmark itself is so small and flat that biking between the school and city is common for students. A decent bike will run you back about 1000DK, but it is a worthwhile investment for the exchange and you can usually sell it to an incoming student. For all you short people out there, you might be able to get away with children's bikes as the Danes are very tall!"
    - Zoe Yu '19

  • University of Sydney:
    Fall Transfer Program

    "Sydney does coffee differently than in the US and it's defintely something to get used to. we were there during summer time so naturally wanted our drinks to be iced. whenever we would order an iced coffee it would come with coffee, sugar/sweetening up, ice cream, ice and whipped cream. if you like dessert for breakfast then you're in the clear but otherwise you're better off ordering a hot drink or making your own iced drinks."


    - Kate Christie '21

    China Winterim Program


    "If you're trying to do some shopping, 50% is not enough, cut the price by 75%, and you're still probably getting ripped off. But hey, it's a win-win on both ends. Also, the kids are going to want to add you on wechat! Add them, they're so great."
